Big Red Ambassadors - Cornell Days Chair

BRAM is Cornell's forefront ambassador group that consists of undergraduates from all majors and hometowns. We work under the guidance of the Undergraduate Admissions Office to serve as a liaison between current students and prospective students and their families.

As the Cornell Days Chair, I have accomplished the following:

  • Pivot to a virtual admitted students programming during the pandemic and identify new opportunities for our ambassadors to engage with over 1,800 high schoolers (largest in the Ivy League)
  • Work with staff at the UAO to plan an in-person Cornell Days where we welcomed 1,000 admitted students and their families to campus in Spring 2022
  • Oversee Cornell Admissions' inbox by managing our ETeam (a group of 10 ambassadors) through developing training material and introducing new processes to improve response time between each inquiry that we received
  • Presented information session on student experience and residential life for 20 students at Herricks High School (Long Island) in Winter 2019.


Association of CS Undergraduates - Corporate Chair
January 2020 - Present

The Association of Computer Science Undergraduates (ACSU) is Cornell's chapter of the Association for Computing Machinery. Our primary purpose is to enhance the undergraduate experience in computer science. We do this by promoting professional, social, and educational interaction among students interested in computers or computer science, as well as facilitating students' communication with CS faculty, alumni, and corporate representatives. The corporate sub-team is responsible for hosting information sessions for companies of all sizes from the tech industry and beyond, which provide a great opportunity for members to learn about employment options and meet recruiters.
