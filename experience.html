Experience

Here is what I have been working on.

Watercooler Project

This is the current project I am working on with two PhD candidates in Policy Analysis and Management and Industrial Psychology. We are trying to learn more about involuntary layoff events - more specifically, our end goal is to create a predictive model using public data (though we're not sure if this is feasible just yet).

So far, I was able to investigate different sources that we could collect our data from. Once I conducted tests to determine the data's representativeness, I wrote queries to extract them. To prepare the data needed for analysis, I am currently working on writing a pipeline by using R to clean up the data.



Trifecta of Social Justice, Engineering, and Communication

This project was done as part of my Engineering Communications course. In my paper, I focused on the triangulation of the three factors and how they all interplay with each other. As part of this exploration, I wanted to analyze three different companies to see how they contribute to the normality of "objective" work in the engineering field. From this research, I learned about the importance in ethics and how there is a continued need to "do the right thing" as we innovate.



BrightInsight

BrightInsight provides the leading global regulated digital health platform for biopharma and medtech. Our medical-grade Internet of Things (IoT) platform is built under a Quality Management System to support and optimize regulated drugs, devices and software through integrated data and actionable insights to enable customers to drive increased patient adherence and engagement.

Summer 2021 Internship
This summer I worked under the Engineering and Data Science Team as a Program Management Intern. I was the Scrum Master for the Data Engineering Team, which consisted of 17 engineers. Here, I lead Scrum ceremonies such as daily stand ups and sprint retrospectives. Additionally, I worked with stakeholders to create and maintain a project schedule in order to track its health. I also wrote tutorials that detailed best Agile practices as well as technical documentations for using certain platforms. Lastly, I used my statistics knowledge to investigate the data collected from Jira during the team's sprints in order to determine how to make the team more efficient.

BrightInsight provided such a welcoming culture as I enjoyed doing virtual coffee chats with various team members here. This was an excellent learning experience for me and I'm very thankful to have had the opportunity to intern here. Special shoutout to the PMO Team since I worked with them on a daily basis and had many valuable takeaways!


Blackjack - CS3110 Final Project

For the final project in my functional programming course, my partner and I decided to create a game of Blackjack from scratch using OCaml. It was valuable to go through the full Software Development Lifecycle for the first time. We decided to use an Agile approach to our development as we came up with playable code each time. With each sprint, we were able to add more features by talking to our friends and learning about what would make the experience more exciting. Additionally, I got to practice designing tasks that had to be done through the use of a Trello board. Breaking functionalities down into individual tickets helped me understand what the code needed to do and how everything eventually fitted together.

Housing Inequality - PAM2070 Policy Brief

As part of the course "Big Data for Big Policy Problems," we had to write a mock policy brief to a local representative. I chose to focus mine on housing inequaity in Nassau County and applied concepts that we learned throughout the class. More importantly, I got a better glimpse of what it is like being a data scientist. This brief gave me the chance to create visuals through R's tidyverse package to help support my argument. I learned that this was something I enjoyed, so I can see myself doing more projects that emphasize this skill in the future!
